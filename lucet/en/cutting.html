Cutting a lucet cord neatly

Front page Basic luceting Tips Links FAQ Fingerlooping course

If you cut into a luceted cord, it starts unraveling if the end isn't finished somehow. You can of course knot the end or dip it in glue.

Jos haarukkanyörin katkaisee, se alkaa purkautua, ellei sitä viimeistellä jotenkin. Nyörin päähän voi tietenkin tehdä solmun tai pään voi kastaa liimaan. Common Whipping is a slightly more elegant way of finishing the cut end. But if your yarn is a bit thicker (or you have nimble fingers and a good eyesight), you can also finish the cut ends the same way you end a cord when luceting.

Usually it is easier to make a cord to the correct measure than to fiddle with it afterwards. If you need 3 x 1.5 meters of cord, it is a good idea to make three 1.5 meter cords rather than one 5 meter long, unless you really enjoy fiddling with the cut ends.

For cutting and finishing you need a cord to be cut, scissors, two safety pins (or openable stitch markers) and a pin (if the yarn is thin) or a darning needle or a thin knitting needle (if the yarn is thicker); in any case something small and fairly sharp. Even a cocktail stick or a fork may do in a pinch.

If your object is to create a given length, mark somehow (for example with a third safety pin) the point where the cord should end. If you cut a cord into several shorter cords, note that cutting and finishing shortens the end by about 5 cm.

Cut the cord with scissors. If you need to get a certain length, cut 5 cm above the marked ending point.
Take a cut end and the pin/needle and use it to pick out all the loose yarn ends. After that your cord end should have two small loops, one slightly lower than the other, and a yarn end. Pull gently the yarn end so that the cord unravels a bit and the yarn end gets longer. If you are making a certain length, unravel until you reach the marker for the end point.
Place the cord on the table as if it was on a lucet. On the left the loop that is slightly higher up, on the right a loop and a yarn (the one you just pulled). Place a safety pin/stitch marker through the loop on the right.
Gently pull the left loop bigger. Try to get the extra yarn from the yarn end, not from the right loop. Fasten the other safety pin through the left loop you just made bigger.
Gently pull the right loop bigger. The extra yarn comes from the left loop, try not to pull it too small (although the safety pin will stop you from pulling too far).
Remove the safety pin from the right loop. Take the yarn end and put it through the right loop.
Pull the front side of the left loop so that the right loop tightens around the yarn end.
Remove the safety pin from the left loop. Put the yarn end through the loop. Pull the yarn end until the left loop tightens around the yarn.
You have finished off the end and it won't unravel.

Repeat with the other cut cord end.
